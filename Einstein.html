Einstein and the Creative Mind

Wake up!

Wake Up Your Creative Mind!

In this interactive, music-filled children’s musical, Albert Einstein himself arrives—wild hair, violin, and all—to help kids discover the power of imagination. Einstein and the Creative Mind invites young audiences on a lively journey through four colorful stories: a boy who turns invisible, an opera singer’s funny rivalry, a wish gone hilariously wrong, and a dragon with the worst breath in the kingdom.

Packed with original songs, live music, and laugh-out-loud moments, the show turns the audience into participants, letting kids solve problems, offer advice, and even help Einstein conduct a concert. Along the way, children learn that creativity isn’t just about art or music; it’s about seeing the world differently and believing they can make it better.

Designed for ages 8–12 but fun for the whole family, Einstein and the Creative Mind brings science, music, and imagination together in a delightful hour that will leave everyone inspired to keep dreaming—and creating.

Workshop Production

Take a peek into the first workshop production of Einstein and the Creative Mind, written by Giovanni Cipolla and Mark Snyder. This interactive, music-filled show featured Scott Boring as Einstein, with David Mooney, Tina Van Osten, Kim Adler, and Dave Gold as the Players. Directed by Charles Gill and music directed by Christine CS Walker, the production was produced by Jenn Cipolla with Little Sandbox Players.
